## S2: Summary of Subsections in the "AUGMENTATION PROCESS IN RAG"

The section "Augmentation Process in RAG" includes three critical subsections, each contributing to the overall effectiveness of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). Below is a summary of these subsections:

1. **Iterative Retrieval**: This method enhances information retrieval by repeatedly searching the knowledge base based on the initial user query and the text generated thus far. The goal is to provide large language models (LLMs) with a comprehensive set of contextual references, thereby improving the robustness of the answers generated. An example of this approach is the ITERRETGEN framework, which facilitates continuous refinement of the information retrieved.

2. **Recursive Retrieval**: This approach involves a feedback loop where the results of previous retrievals inform subsequent searches. By refining search queries based on earlier outcomes, recursive retrieval improves the relevance and accuracy of the retrieved data, particularly in complex scenarios. Notable applications of this method include IRCoT and ToC, which help clarify ambiguous queries and enhance the overall search process.

3. **Adaptive Retrieval**: This technique allows models to autonomously determine when and what to retrieve, thereby increasing both efficiency and relevance. The FLARE framework exemplifies adaptive retrieval by monitoring confidence levels during the retrieval process, ensuring that the responses generated are closely aligned with user expectations and requirements.

These subsections collectively enhance the augmentation process by employing distinct strategies to improve the quality and relevance of the information retrieved in RAG.
