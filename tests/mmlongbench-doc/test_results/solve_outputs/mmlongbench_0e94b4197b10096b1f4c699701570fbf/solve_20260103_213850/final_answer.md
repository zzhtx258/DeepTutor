## S1: Definition of the Law of Good Gestalt

The **Law of Good Gestalt** is a foundational principle in Gestalt psychology, which posits that elements of objects are perceptually grouped together when they create a pattern that is regular, simple, and orderly. This principle emphasizes that as individuals perceive their environment, they tend to simplify complex stimuli, focusing on overall patterns rather than individual components. 

According to this law, the human perceptual system prioritizes global regularity over local irregularities, allowing for a more coherent understanding of visual information. This means that when faced with a complex scene, individuals will instinctively organize the elements into a unified whole, thereby enhancing their ability to interpret and navigate their surroundings effectively. The emphasis on conciseness is a key aspect of Gestalt theory, highlighting the tendency to perceive the simplest and most organized form of reality available to us [rag-1][rag-2][rag-3].

In summary, the Law of Good Gestalt illustrates how our perception is shaped by our innate desire to create order and simplicity from the complexity of the world around us.

This process not only enhances clarity but also aids in the understanding of complex scenes and objects, as it minimizes extraneous details that could otherwise obscure meaning.

Moreover, the law highlights the human inclination to see patterns and structures in visual stimuli, which is a fundamental aspect of how we engage with the world. By focusing on simplicity and order, the Law of Good Gestalt illustrates our psychological drive to create meaning from the complexity surrounding us, reinforcing the idea that perception is not merely a passive reception of stimuli but an active process of organization and interpretation [rag-4].

In summary, the Law of Good Gestalt encapsulates the principles of perceptual organization, underscoring our innate ability to simplify and structure complex visual information into coherent wholes, thereby facilitating a clearer understanding of our environment.
